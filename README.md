## Overview
This project focuses on analyzing the Titanic dataset to understand passenger demographics and survival behavior. An interactive Excel dashboard was developed to transform raw passenger data into meaningful visual insights for analytical and business-style understanding.

The dashboard highlights how factors such as gender, age, and passenger class influenced survival outcomes.

---

## Project Objective
The primary goals of this project are:

- Analyze overall passenger survival distribution  
- Identify survival differences by gender  
- Examine the impact of passenger class on survival  
- Analyze age-group survival patterns  
- Provide clear, interactive, data-driven insights

## Key Insights

### Survival Overview
- Total passengers analyzed: **1,309**  
- Overall survival rate is significantly lower than non-survival rate.  
- Majority of passengers did not survive the disaster.

### Gender-Based Survival
- **Female passengers** had a much higher survival rate.  
- **Male passengers** experienced the majority of deaths.  
- This reflects the historical “women and children first” evacuation policy.

### Class-Based Analysis
- **Lower-class passengers** had the highest number of passengers and deaths.  
- **First-class passengers** had better survival chances.  
- Passenger class strongly influenced survival probability.

### Age Group Patterns
- The **19–29 years** age group has the highest passenger count.  
- Younger children show relatively better survival chances than adult males.  
- Elderly passenger counts are very low in the dataset.

### Survival by Age
- Survival rates vary across age groups.  
- Children and younger passengers generally had improved survival outcomes compared to middle-aged males.

## Conclusion
The Titanic dataset reveals strong survival disparities based on **gender**, **passenger class**, and **age**. Female and first-class passengers had significantly higher survival probabilities, while lower-class male passengers were the most vulnerable group.

These insights demonstrate how demographic and socioeconomic factors can heavily influence survival outcomes in crisis situations.

Businesses and analysts can use similar analytical approaches to:

- Identify high-risk customer segments  
- Perform demographic behavior analysis  
- Support data-driven strategic decisions
